## Benchmark Interpretation Guidelines

### When to Flag Metrics as Concerning vs Acceptable

**Critical (immediate attention required):**
- Conversion rate below 50% of the vertical low end benchmark
- CPA more than 2x the vertical low end benchmark
- ROAS below 50% of the vertical low end benchmark
- Quality Score averaging 3 or below
- Bounce rate 20+ percentage points above the vertical low end benchmark
- EMQ score below 4.0

**Concerning (investigate within a week):**
- Metrics sitting at or near the low end of vertical benchmarks
- CTR trending downward for 3+ consecutive weeks
- CPC climbing more than 20% without corresponding conversion rate improvement
- Frequency exceeding thresholds for cold audiences
- Landing page time on page below 30 seconds

**Acceptable (monitor normally):**
- Metrics within the benchmark range for the vertical
- Short term fluctuations that stay within 15% of established baselines
- New campaigns in their first 2 weeks of learning phase

### Account Maturity Adjustments

**New accounts (0 to 3 months):**
- Expect CPA to be 30 to 50% higher than benchmarks during the learning phase
- Conversion rates will be 20 to 40% lower until algorithms optimize
- Do not compare directly to top quartile benchmarks; focus on trajectory
- Quality Scores will start low; 4 to 5 is acceptable for the first 60 days

**Established accounts (3 to 12 months):**
- Should be at or above the low end of vertical benchmarks
- If still below low end benchmarks, there are likely structural or tracking issues
- Begin comparing to the mid range of benchmarks

**Mature accounts (12+ months with consistent spend):**
- Should be trending toward top quartile on at least 2 to 3 key metrics
- If all metrics are stuck at low end, there are likely strategic issues holding the account back
- These accounts should show clear gains from historical optimization

### Seasonal Adjustment Notes

**Q4 (October through December):**
- Ecommerce CPCs and CPMs rise 25 to 60% due to holiday competition
- Expect ROAS to compress in November and early December, then spike during peak shopping days
- Do not panic about rising costs in October; judge performance on ROAS, not CPC
- Black Friday through Cyber Monday benchmarks are their own category; do not compare to normal periods

**Q1 (January through March):**
- Legal (especially personal injury) sees peak search volume in January and February
- Fitness, health, and education verticals see surges in January
- Ecommerce CPMs drop significantly in January; use this window for prospecting heavy spend
- Q1 is the best time to scale cold audiences on Meta and TikTok due to lower competition

**Q2 (April through June):**
- Home services peak season begins (HVAC, roofing, landscaping)
- Real estate search volume peaks in spring
- B2B often sees budget releases after Q1 planning; expect increased competition in April and May
- Wedding and event related verticals peak

**Q3 (July through September):**
- Back to school drives education vertical spending
- Ecommerce begins ramping Q4 prep; September CPMs start climbing
- Legal sees summer driving season increase for personal injury
- Use August and September for creative testing before Q4

**Cross seasonal notes:**
- Always compare to the same period in the prior year, not the prior month, when seasonality is a factor
- New client accounts without historical data should be compared to vertical benchmarks with seasonal context noted
- If a client insists on month over month comparisons during seasonal transitions, always include the seasonal context in reporting
