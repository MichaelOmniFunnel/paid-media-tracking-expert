# OFM Competitive Analysis Framework

## When to Use

Run competitive analysis during new client onboarding, quarterly reviews, or when a client's performance deviates significantly from benchmarks. Competitive analysis is not a standalone deliverable in most cases; it feeds into audit reports, onboarding documents, and strategic recommendations.

---

## Google Ads Competitor Research

### Step 1: Auction Insights

Pull Auction Insights at the campaign, ad group, or keyword level depending on the granularity needed.

**What to look for:**
- **Impression Share:** Your client's share vs top competitors. If a competitor has 80%+ impression share on core terms, they are likely using aggressive bidding or very high budgets.
- **Overlap Rate:** How often a competitor's ad shows alongside yours. High overlap (60%+) on branded terms may indicate competitor conquest campaigns.
- **Outranking Share:** How often you rank above vs below a specific competitor. If a competitor outranks you 70%+ of the time, investigate their bidding strategy, Quality Score, and ad relevance.
- **Position Above Rate:** Specifically useful for understanding who consistently takes the top spot.
- **Top of Page Rate and Absolute Top of Page Rate:** Tells you if competitors are paying for premium positions.

**How to interpret:**
- A competitor with high impression share but low outranking share is spending big but not efficiently. They may be vulnerable.
- A competitor with high outranking share and high top of page rate is strong; focus on differentiation rather than outbidding.
- Look at Auction Insights trends over time (use the date range selector). A competitor suddenly appearing or increasing share may signal a new campaign launch.

### Step 2: Google Ads Transparency Center

Go to adstransparency.google.com and search by advertiser name or domain.

**What to look for:**
- Active ad copy and messaging angles
- Offer positioning (discounts, free consultations, guarantees)
- Ad formats being used (text, responsive, call only)
- Geographic targeting patterns (which regions they are advertising in)
- How frequently they refresh ad copy

### Step 3: Search Term Analysis

Review your client's search terms report and cross reference with Auction Insights.

**What to infer:**
- If a competitor shows up on broad or generic terms, they are likely using broad match or Performance Max with wide targeting.
- If a competitor only shows on high intent terms, they are running tighter keyword strategies.
- Identify terms where competitors appear that your client is not bidding on; these may be expansion opportunities.

### Step 4: SERP Analysis

Manually search for 10 to 15 core keywords in an incognito window (set the correct location via Google Ads Preview Tool or a geo proxy).

**Document:**
- Which competitors appear consistently in paid results
- What ad extensions they use (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, call extensions, location extensions)
- Their messaging themes and unique selling propositions
- Whether they are running Google Guaranteed or Local Service Ads
- Organic positioning of the same competitors (strong organic + paid presence means a well funded competitor)

## Meta Ads Competitor Research

### Step 1: Meta Ad Library

Go to facebook.com/ads/library. Search by advertiser name or keyword.

**What to look for:**
- **Active ads count:** A high number of active ads (50+) suggests heavy testing. A low number (under 10) may mean limited budget or a set it and forget it approach.
- **Ad formats:** Are they running static images, carousels, video, collection ads, instant experiences? Variety suggests a mature media buying operation.
- **Messaging themes:** Identify 3 to 5 core messaging angles (price, quality, social proof, urgency, education, emotional appeal).
- **Offer types:** Free trials, discounts, lead magnets, free consultations, gated content.
- **Creative refresh frequency:** Scroll through the "Started running" dates. If all ads started around the same time, they are not refreshing. If there is a steady stream of new ads, they are actively testing.
- **Landing page patterns:** Click through ads to see where they send traffic. Document URL structure, page layout, offer, and conversion mechanism.

### Step 2: Audience Inference

You cannot see a competitor's targeting directly, but you can infer it from their creative.

**Signals to look for:**
- Ad creative that speaks to specific demographics ("Moms in Austin," "First time homebuyers")
- Retargeting signals (ads that reference "Come back," "Still thinking about it," "You left something behind")
- Lookalike indicators (broad creative with wide appeal suggests LAL or broad targeting)
- Sequential messaging (educational content followed by direct response ads suggests a funnel approach)

### Step 3: Document Patterns

For each competitor, note:
- Primary platform focus (heavy on Meta vs Google vs both)
- Creative production quality (professional vs UGC vs mixed)
- Estimated budget tier (based on ad library volume and running duration)
- Key differentiators they emphasize

## TikTok Ads Competitor Research

### Step 1: TikTok Creative Center

Go to ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter. Use the Top Ads dashboard.

**What to look for:**
- Top performing ad formats in the vertical (What length? What style? What hooks?)
- Trending sounds or music being used in ads
- Common patterns in the first 3 seconds (the hook determines everything on TikTok)
- Whether competitors are using spark ads (boosted organic content) vs traditional in feed ads

### Step 2: TikTok Ad Library

Search by advertiser name or keyword in the TikTok Ad Library (library.tiktok.com).

**Document:**
- Active ad creative
- Duration each ad has been running (longer running ads are likely performing well)
- Creative style (professional production vs creator content vs mix)
- Call to action patterns
- Landing page destinations

### Step 3: Creative Analysis

For TikTok specifically, analyze:
- **Hook patterns:** How do the first 1 to 3 seconds grab attention? (text overlay, surprising visual, question, bold claim)
- **Format:** Talking head, product demo, before/after, testimonial, trend based, screen recording
- **Pacing:** Fast cuts vs slow narrative; what seems to work in the vertical
- **Audio:** Voiceover, trending sounds, original audio, text to speech
- **CTA placement:** In the video itself vs relying on the platform CTA button

## Landing Page Competitive Analysis

### Step 1: Visit Competitor Landing Pages from Their Ads

Click through competitor ads on each platform. Document the destination URL and take screenshots.

### Step 2: Evaluate Each Landing Page

For each competitor landing page, assess:

**Offer Positioning:**
- What is the primary offer? (discount, free trial, free consultation, instant quote)
- How prominently is the offer displayed?
- Is there a secondary offer for visitors who are not ready to convert?

**Trust Elements:**
- Reviews and testimonials (how many, how prominent)
- Logos of partners, certifications, media mentions
- Guarantees (money back, satisfaction, results)
- Social proof counters ("10,000+ customers served")

**Form and Conversion Mechanism:**
- Number of form fields
- Multi step vs single step form
- Phone number prominence (click to call)
- Chat widget presence
- Exit intent popup

**Page Experience:**
- Load time (test with PageSpeed Insights)
- Mobile responsiveness (test on actual mobile device)
- Above the fold content (what do they prioritize)
- Page length and scroll depth
- Video usage

### Step 3: Screenshot and Document

Save screenshots of each competitor's key landing pages. These will be referenced in the competitive analysis report and can help inform creative recommendations for your client.

## Deliverable Template

When producing a competitive analysis report, use this structure:

### 1. Executive Summary of Competitive Landscape
- Brief overview of the competitive environment (2 to 3 paragraphs max)
- Key takeaway: is the market saturated, is there whitespace, are competitors sophisticated or basic?
- Overall competitive threat level: Low, Moderate, High

### 2. Competitor Profiles (Top 3 to 5)

For each competitor:
- **Company name and URL**
- **Estimated ad spend level:** Low (under $5k/mo), Moderate ($5k to $25k/mo), High ($25k to $100k/mo), Very High ($100k+/mo)
- **Platforms active on:** Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, other
- **Primary messaging angle**
- **Creative sophistication:** Basic, Moderate, Advanced
- **Key strength**
- **Key vulnerability**

### 6. Opportunities Identified from Competitor Gaps

Bulleted list of specific, actionable opportunities. Each opportunity should tie to a gap in competitor coverage, messaging, or execution.

Examples of good opportunities:
- "No competitor is running TikTok ads; first mover advantage available"
- "Competitor A's landing page loads in 8+ seconds; we can win on page experience"
- "All competitors lead with discount messaging; we can differentiate with value/quality messaging"

### 7. Defensive Recommendations Where Competitors Are Strong

Bulleted list of areas where competitors are doing well and recommendations for how to defend or match.

Examples:
- "Competitor B has 85% impression share on branded terms; consider a branded defense campaign"
- "Competitor C has a strong UGC creative program; recommend testing UGC approach to compete"
- "Multiple competitors are using retargeting aggressively; ensure we have full funnel coverage"
